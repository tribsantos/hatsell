# Hatsell for the Chair

You're running the meeting. That might sound intimidating — but Hatsell does the heavy lifting. You don't need to memorize parliamentary procedure. You just need to follow the prompts.

This tutorial walks you through creating and running a complete meeting, from setup to adjournment.

---

## Part 1: Before the meeting

### Create the meeting

1. Open Hatsell and enter your name.
2. Tap **Create a New Meeting**.
3. You'll land on the General Settings screen. Fill in what you know — everything has sensible defaults:

**Organization Profile**
- **Organization name** — shows in the top bar during the meeting (e.g., "Maplewood Neighborhood Association").
- **Total membership** — used to calculate quorum. Leave blank if you're not sure.
- **Quorum** — the minimum number of members needed to do business. The default (majority of membership) follows Robert's Rules. You can set a fixed number or fraction if your bylaws say something different.
- **Voting basis** — how votes are counted. The default ("votes cast") is correct for most organizations. Only change this if your bylaws specify otherwise.
- **Debate limits** — how long each person can speak (default: 10 minutes) and how many times (default: 2). You can also set a total debate time limit per motion.

**Settings for this meeting**
- **Meeting name** — "Regular Meeting," "Annual Meeting," etc.
- **Agenda** — optionally add agenda items. You can use them as guidance (a reference for yourself) or as adopted Orders of the Day (binding).
- **Previous notice** — check any items that members were notified about in advance. This affects what vote thresholds apply.
- **Meeting type** — regular or special. Special meetings can only discuss business specified in the call.

Don't overthink the settings. The defaults work for a standard meeting. You can always come back and adjust for next time.

### Share the codes

4. Tap **Confirm**. Hatsell generates a unique code for each role: Chair, Vice Chair, Secretary, and Member.
5. Share the appropriate code with each person. The **Member** code is the one most people need. You can tap **Copy All Codes** to paste them into a group chat or email.
6. Tap **Start Meeting** when you're ready.

## Part 2: Running the meeting

Once the meeting starts, you'll see three things that will be your constant companions:

- **The guidance box** (red left border) — tells you what to say and what to do next. This is your script.
- **The chair actions** — prominent buttons for whatever decision the meeting needs from you right now.
- **The top bar** — shows the meeting stage, organization name, and drawer toggles for the participant list, speaking queue, and meeting log.

### Call to order

The guidance box shows your opening script. Tap **Call to Order**. That's it — the meeting has begun.

### Roll call

The secretary (or you, if there's no secretary) calls each member's name by tapping their card. Members tap **Present** on their own screens. Once everyone has responded, you'll see a button to **Complete Roll Call**.

If you set a quorum, Hatsell tells you whether quorum is met. If it's not, you can't proceed to business — the app will guide you.

### Approve minutes

If this isn't your first meeting, the guidance prompts you to ask for approval of the previous meeting's minutes. Members can submit corrections. You can accept corrections by general consent or put them to a vote.

When minutes are approved (or if there are no minutes to approve), you move on.

### New business

This is where the real work happens. The flow:

1. **A member makes a motion.** They tap their "Make a Motion" button and type what they propose. You'll see it appear as a pending motion.
2. **You recognize it.** Tap the motion to bring it to the floor. (If it's clearly out of order, you can reject it with an explanation.)
3. **Another member seconds.** The motion needs a second before debate can begin. If nobody seconds, you declare "no second" and the motion dies.
4. **You state the question.** The guidance gives you the script: "It is moved and seconded that [motion text]. Is there any debate?"
5. **Members debate.** They join the speaking queue by tapping "Speak in Favor" or "Speak Against." You recognize them in order. The speaking timer tracks their time.
6. **You call the vote.** When debate is done (or a member moves to close debate), tap **Call the Vote**. Members vote Aye, Nay, or Abstain on their screens.
7. **You announce the result.** Hatsell counts the votes and tells you whether the motion passed or failed. Tap **Announce Result** and the guidance gives you the script.

Repeat for each item of business.

### Handling interruptions

During business, members can raise:
- **Point of order** — a member thinks the rules are being broken. You'll see it immediately and must rule on it.
- **Parliamentary inquiry** — a member has a question about procedure. Answer it and move on.
- **Amendments** — a member wants to modify the motion on the floor. Amendments are debated and voted on before returning to the main motion.
- **Privileged motions** — a member moves to recess or to adjourn. These take priority over regular business.

Don't worry about remembering which of these is allowed when. Hatsell only shows members the options that are currently in order. If a button appears, they can use it.

### Adjourn

When there's no further business, tap **Adjourn** (or a member can move to adjourn). The meeting ends. The log contains a complete record of everything that happened.

## Part 3: During the meeting — your toolkit

### The drawers

Tap the buttons in the top bar to open:
- **Members** — who's connected, their roles, and whether quorum is met. You can also set quorum here if you didn't set it during setup.
- **Queue** — the speaking queue, showing who's waiting and their stance (for/against).
- **Log** — a running record of every action taken in the meeting.

### What the guidance box does for you

The guidance box updates at every stage and transition. It tells you:
- What to say out loud (in italic script text)
- What button to press next
- What the members are waiting for

If you ever feel lost, read the guidance box. It always knows where you are.

### When a member does something unexpected

Parliamentary procedure has a response for everything. If a member raises a point of order, the guidance will tell you how to rule. If someone moves to table the motion, the app walks you through it. If an appeal is made against your ruling, Hatsell handles the vote.

You don't need to anticipate these situations. Just respond when they come up — the app will guide you through.

## Quick reference

| Stage | What you do |
|-------|-------------|
| Call to Order | Tap the button, read the script |
| Roll Call | Wait for members to respond, then complete |
| Minutes | Ask for corrections, approve |
| Business | Recognize motions, manage debate, call votes, announce results |
| Adjourn | Tap Adjourn or recognize a motion to adjourn |

---

## Tips

- **You don't need to talk fast.** Parliamentary procedure is designed to be deliberate. Take your time.
- **Read the scripts out loud.** They're there for a reason — they set expectations for the members.
- **Use the speaking timer.** It keeps debate fair without you having to be the bad guy.
- **Don't skip roll call** for formal meetings. Quorum matters — it protects decisions from being challenged later.
- **Check the log** if you lose track. It records everything.
- **Your first meeting will feel slow.** That's normal. By the third meeting, the flow will feel natural.
